The Revival of Vanderbilt Unicef

  • Daniel Yakubu
  • Mar 18, 2023

The Vanderbilt UNICEF chapter was once a thriving organization on campus, but in recent years it has become inactive essentially dead. This was due, in part, to the Covid-19 pandemic. However, we have taken on the challenge of resurrecting Vanderbilt UNICEF.

We recognize and resonate with UNICEF's mission to improve the lives of children around the World and so we are committed to making sure we can contributr through Vanderbilt Unicef.

We recognize and resonate with UNICEF's mission to improve the lives of children around the world.

To begin the revival of UNICEF on campus, we are reaching out to the university and the community to gauge interest and recruit new members. We are also working to establish partnerships with local businesses and organizations to help raise awareness and support for UNICEF's programs.

In addition to building a strong membership base, we are planning a series of events and initiatives to engage the campus community and raise funds for UNICEF. These activities will range from charity runs and benefit concerts to educational seminars and service trips.

The journey to rebuild Vanderbilt UNICEF will certainly be challenging, but the thought of bringing happiness and comfort to children around the world keeps us going.

As we work to revive the organization, we hope to inspire others to get involved in efforts to improve the lives of children around the world. We believe that by coming together and working towards a common goal, we can create real change and make a difference in the lives of those who need it most.

Join us to make a change, because Every Child Matters.
